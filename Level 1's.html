Level 1's

Getting level 1 cerified was one of the biggest achivements of my life. Becoming a level 1 certified instructor means that U.S. Sailing nationally recognizes you as a certified sailor, who is allowed to teach anyone the fundamentals of sailing.

To become a certified instructor, you must pass a 4 day, 40 hour course. This course includes 3 written exams, along with on the water leaership, and teamwork skills.

The written exams tested our knowledge of:

  • Motorboat parts and safety
  • Fundamentals of Sailing
  • On the water safety, and sailboat rescue procedures

On all of these tests, you must score an 80 or above to pass the course.

The on the water tests included:

  • A capsize drill
  • A follow the leader type drill, where you were in a motorboat and led the class around the harbor
  • And finally, you had to come up with a drill on your own, and lead the rest of the class while out on the water

    • Along with all these tests and drills, there were presentations we gave, and public speeches we had to make. We also had to know some basic knot tying including: a figure 8, a bowline, and how to tie off a boat to a dock.
